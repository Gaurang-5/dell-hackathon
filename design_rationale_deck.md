# Design Rationale Deck

Use one section per slide. This deck has ten slides.

## Slide 1 — TrustOps AI

**Transparent and trustworthy AI agent interfaces**

An IT operations prototype that explains recommendations clearly and keeps people in control of every consequential action.

## Slide 2 — The trust problem

IT teams receive urgent recommendations without enough context to decide safely. The result is delay, blind approval, or unnecessary overrides.

## Slide 3 — Who we designed for

- **IT Administrator:** needs fast, accountable operational decisions.
- **Security Analyst:** needs evidence, alternatives, and a review trail.
- **Business stakeholder:** needs a plain-language view of current operational impact.

## Slide 4 — One workflow, five connected views

Command Center, Leadership Summary, Device Fleet, Security Events, and Audit Log support review from alert to accountable decision.

## Slide 5 — What makes a recommendation understandable

Every recommendation shows what was found, why it matters, what is recommended, what information supported it, and where the system has limitations.

## Slide 6 — Human control is the safety boundary

Approve, Override, Ask Why, See Alternatives, and Escalate to Human Review are distinct controls. A second confirmation is required before a decision is recorded.

## Slide 7 — Provenance without live data

Every source is identified as a Faker-generated simulated log from the fixed June 2024 demo dataset. The prototype does not connect to live devices or external services.

## Slide 8 — Accountability after the decision

Confirmed actions enter a searchable, filterable audit history with the device, action, reason, timestamp, and human decision. Incident reports preserve context when someone overrides or escalates.

## Slide 9 — What we learned from usability sessions

Five think-aloud sessions showed that people could identify the recommendation, supporting evidence, limitation, and required human decision. Participants asked for stronger alternative-action visibility, which informed the control layout.

## Slide 10 — Trust is a product behavior

TrustOps AI does not ask people to accept an unexplained action. It provides understandable evidence, admits limits, records human choices, and never changes a device without approval.
